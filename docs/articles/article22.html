Article: Protecting Your Intellectual Property As a content creator, it is important to protect your intellectual property. This includes ensuring that you have the right to use and distribute your work without infringement on someone else's copyright. One way to do this is by obtaining a copyright for your work. This allows you to control who can use and distribute your work, as well as any rights of public display, performance, or distribution. However, there are certain limitations to copyright protection. For example, fair use provisions allow for limited use of copyrighted material for purposes such as criticism, commentary, news reporting, teaching, scholarship, or research. Additionally, the First Amendment protects freedom of expression, so even if a work is protected by copyright, it may still be used in certain contexts without infringement. It is important to understand these limitations and to seek legal advice if you are unsure about whether your use of someone else's work is infringing on their copyright. By taking steps to protect your intellectual property, you can ensure that your work is properly credited and that you are able to control who can use it.
